About Student Week

Welcome To InPTA Student Week 2023

InPTA hosts a student week designed to offer an introduction and guidance for those who are interested in PTA science and GW searches within PTAs. This student week comprises three phases, each spanning two to three days. In the first phase, students receive an introduction to pulsars and the fundamentals of pulsar observations with uGMRT. The second phase provides an introduction to pulsar timing techniques, PTAs, and offers insights into the work conducted at InPTA. In the third phase, participants engage in discussions about recent PTA findings, the methods employed in GW searches, and the potential of PTA datasets to unlock and understand new physics phenomena.

InPTA

Indian Pulsar Timing Array Experiment (InPTA) is a pulsar timing experiment searching for low frequency nano-hertz Gravitational Waves and is operational since 2016. InPTA is an Indo-Japanese collaboration which makes use of the unique capabilities of the upgraded Giant Meterwave Radio Telescope (uGMRT) for monitoring a sample of nearby millisecond pulsars for this purpose. InPTA is a member of International Pulsar Timing collaboration (IPTA).
